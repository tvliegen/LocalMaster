Introduction                                                                       

Although Teknion always strives to provide our customers with the highest quality products possible, when, where and how our customers need them, we recognize that sometimes things can go wrong.  To help resolve these issues as quickly and painlessly as possible, Teknion is pleased to provide the online Tekcare Dealer Portal.  The Tekcare Dealer Portal provides a one-stop-shop for all aspects of claims resolution.  In the portal, dealers are able to:

·         Launch claims

·         Provide additional information to support existing claims

·         Communicate directly with Teknion staff tasked with resolving issues

·         Track claim status

·         Manage installer field reports

·         Enter back charge requests, and more.

This document provides step-by-step directions on navigating the Portal and completing the key claims functions.

The Navigating the Dealer Portal

To access the Tekcare Dealer Portal, you will need a login and password.  If your dealership has already been set-up with an admin account, then contact your local admin to provide you with credentials associated with your dealership.  If you do not have a local admin, you can contact a Teknion Corporate Claims representative at &&&&&&&&&&&@teknion.com to have your dealership set-up.

Once you have credentials, navigate to http://teknion.herokuapp.com/.

 

When prompted, enter your username and password.  From this landing page, you will see a high level dashboard of your current claims.  See Figure 1 and Table 1 for a description of the key information available on the dashboard.

Answering and Requesting Clarifications

Electronic communications required to further your claim will be conducted online via the Dealer Portal.  For urgent claims related issues, dealers are still able to call the Teknion Corporate Claims Group at X-XXX-XXX-XXXX however, for all standard communications, you are encouraged to use the more efficient Dealer Portal. 

If there is an outstanding clarification request, you will see this at both the Claim Number level and the Issue level.   On the dealer dashboard (the Claim Number level), you will know that a claim has an outstanding clarification request or an open site visit request because the status will be listed as Communication Pending.   To determine the specifics of the request, expand the Communication Pending phrase to see the issue level detail.  Click the issue number to go directly to the issue.

Figure 4:  Communication Pending

On the claim detail screen, you will see all issues related to the claim, along with their status.  Those requiring clarifications will be in the status Clarification Requested.  Click on the issue number to drill down into the issue and provide clarification.

 

Figure 5:  Claim Detail Screen for Clarification Requested

 

On the issue detail screen, you can click on the clarification tab to see the outstanding clarification.  Note that although there may be multiple clarifications related to a given claim, only one can be open at any given time and it can be open for response from either Teknion or a dealer.  Previously answered clarifications will still be available for reference.  Once you have identified the outstanding request, as noted by the status Awaiting Response, click on the date to respond to the request.

 

Figure 6:  Clarification Request at the Issue Level

 

Figure 7:  Answering a clarification request

Note that when attaching files, it may take up to fifteen minutes for the file to be available in the Dealer Portal and internal Teknion systems.

If you need to request a clarification (i.e. if you have a question about an outstanding claim request) and there are no open clarification requests, you can ask the question from the Issue Detail Clarification tab by clicking the NEW button.  Note that the button is only available if there are no open requests.

Figure 8:  Requesting a clarification

Simply fill in a Subject, ask your question and, upload files as necessary and the Teknion employee responsible for your claim will provide a response in the Dealer Portal.  Be sure to check back for changes in status.

Figure 9:  Making a clarification request

Creating New Claims

New claims are created from the Claim Requests screen in the Dealer Portal.  To access the Claim Requests screen, you can click on the New Claim Requests tab on the main page.  Click on the Tekcare link near the top left of the screen to return to the main screen.

Figure 17:  Accessing the Claim Request Screen

The Claim Requests Screen has four primary purposes:

1.       Beginning the claim process

2.       Managing claims that have not yet been submitted

3.       Submitting claims that are ready to send to Teknion

4.       Viewing and deleting Field Reports generated via the Fotonotes Enterprises mobile app.

 

Initiating a New Claim Request

From the Claim Requests screen, press the Create new button to launch the New Claim Form.

Figure 18:  Launching a new claim

When you launch the new claim form, you will need either the Shipment Number (also known as the Ack#) or the Universal Number.  Without one of these references, you will be unable to begin the claim.

When prompted, select the Shipment or the Universal, as appropriate, enter the reference value and click Look it Up.

Figure 19: Looking up an order

 

Once you have clicked confirm, you will be brought to the New Claim Form, where you will have the opportunity to review the pre-populated order information, as well as enter specific information related to the current claim.  The specific information will include the dealer contact information, the installer, the replacement contact information and the product or process specific details related to the deficiency.  In addition, you can attach files, such as photos, videos, text, and bill of lading documents to further support your claim.

Dealer Information

Certain Dealer Information will be pre-populated based upon your login and the order that was selected.  The remaining mandatory fields need to be completed in order to submit the claim.

See Figure 20 for the required fields.  Note that the CIC Reference Number is currently a customer specific number used only for certain Canadian customers and is NOT a required field.

 

Figure 20:  New claim request - Dealer Information

 

Claim Information

After the dealer information has been completed, you must enter high level information regarding the claim, as in Figure 21.

Figure 21:  New claim request - Claim Information

It is important to correctly select the Claim Type as this will impact the routing of your claim, ensuring the correct Teknion resources are notified of your request.  There are two options to select from:

Claim Type 1:  Product Deficiency and/or Product Received with Visible Damage/Shortage:

·         Select this option if you have a problem (or multiple problems) affecting specific products.  For example, you would select this to note missing hardware, damaged hardware, installation difficulties, warranty claims, specification errors, wrong products, etc.

·         If the issue is due to visible damage or missing product that was identified at delivery, a signed bill of lading is required.

Claim Type 2:  Property Damage or Late Delivery with Appointment

·         Select this option if you have a claim related to property damage caused by the freight carrier or to a late delivery that missed an agreed upon delivery window.  By selecting this option, the claim will be directly routed to the Teknion Logistics Team for resolution.

·         Note that in order for this type of claim to be accepted, you will always need to submit a signed bill of lading noting the issue.

Claim Title:  This field if strictly for the dealer to be able to manage their claims and is not used by Teknion.  Choose a Claim Title that makes sense to you.

Was Product in Teknion Storage:  YES should be selected if the dealer or customer requested Teknion to hold the product for a given time period.  Otherwise, this should be NO.

Shipment Receipt Date:  This is the date the problematic product or service was delivered.

Any field containing REPLACEMENT refers to the replacement order ship-to details and / or contact person where any potential replacement products will be sent.  Although not every claim will require replacements, in order to streamline the process in the event than a replacement is necessary, the REPLACEMENT fields are mandatory.  The Call Before and After Hours fields also refer to the replacement order details.

Please pay particular attention to the Ship Replacement Order Complete selection.  When multiple products on a punch list must be replaced, selecting NO will mean each product will be shipped independently as it is completed.  Selecting YES will hold all products and schedule the replacements based upon the longest lead time product on the punch list.

Additional Special Comments:  This field can be used to provide any additional information that is relevant to the claim, but that is not the deficiency description itself.  For example, if the deficiency is related to product in a brand new show room and the launch of the show room is three weeks away, then providing this detail will help Teknion attempt to meet your timeline.

Installer Information

Please provide the details on the Installation Company, lead installer and contact information.  In addition, by clicking YES for Installation in Process and providing a scheduled completion date, Teknion will do everything in our power to deliver any required replacements prior to the installers leaving the site.

Line Items

Adding lines to the claim is how you will provide specific details on the problem(s) experienced.  If you selected Claim Type 2:  Property Damage or Late Delivery with Appointment, you will see a line for Freight has automatically been added to your claim and you can skip the next step.  If you selected Claim Type 1:  Product Deficiency and/or Product Received with Visible Damage/Shortage, you must select the deficient product(s) and provide details.

Figure 23:  Adding lines to a claim

After adding the lines to a claim, details on the deficiency must be provided.  This can be done in one of two ways:  (i) filling in the required fields manually or (ii) associating a field report, generated on the mobile app, with the item.  In both cases, you will need to open the claim issue line detail box, which only becomes available after items have been added.

 

Figure 24: Adding line details manually

  

Figure 25:  Adding line details from a field report

You must repeat the above process for each line on a claim.

Once all issue lines have been completed, the claim detail status indicated for each line will be a check mark.  If you still see a pencil, refresh your webpage to ensure the changes have been applied.  If you still a pencil, click on it to revisit the detail as a required field is still missing.

If an item was added to a claim in error, it can be deleted by clicking the trash can.

Figure 26:  Completed line items ready for submission

The final step in completing the new claim form is only required if you are making a claim related to freight, in which case a signed bill of lading is required.  For example, this would apply during the following scenarios:

·         Property damaged due to carrier

·         Late carrier

·         Product damaged during transit

·         Items on the shipping manifest are missing at delivery

If any of these reasons apply, then you must upload the bill of lading by clicking on the Upload a File as below. 

Finally, once complete, click Return to go back to the Claim Requests screen.

Figure 27:  Attaching a bill of lading

Submitting a New Claim

Once you have prepared your claim and entered all required information, you still need to submit the claim to Teknion.  This is accomplished from the Claim Requests Screen.  On this screen, you will see all of the claims you have initiated, but not yet submitted.  The Status field will tell you if you have more work to do on a claim, if the claim is ready to be submitted or if the claim has already been sent to Teknion.

Figure 28:  Submitting a new claim request

If the claim request status says New and the green Submit button is active, you have already entered all information required to submit the claim to Teknion.  If you are ready, simply click Submit and the claim will be routed to the appropriate Teknion resources based on the specifics of the claim.

For claims that are either Incomplete or New, but to which you would like to update the content, you can click on the claim request Title to access and change the details.  This functionality allows you to partially complete a claim request and return to it later to finalize.

You may occasionally see a request in Submitted status.  These claim requests have already been sent to Teknion, but have not yet been assigned a Claim Number or migrated to the In Process tab.  This is an automated process and claims will not remain in the submitted status.  Requests cannot be updated once in the Submitted status.

To view all existing field reports that have been created by users within your dealership profile (dealer employees or authorized installers), you can click on the View Field Reports button at the top of the Claims Requests screen.

Using FotoNotes Enterprise  The Teknion Mobile Claims App

Teknion is pleased to be partnering with Fotobabble, Inc., to provide the ability to capture field reports, at the job site, on your mobile phone.  The FotoNotes Enterprise App, available for iPhone or Android devices, gives you the ability to create field reports including:  item numbers; problem descriptions; problem categories; quantities; photos; and voiceovers.

Creating Field Reports

To create a field report in FotoNotes, tap the three circles in the top, right corner of the app.  The next screen will allow you to sort your existing field reports or to launch a new report.  Tap New to begin.

Figure 33:  Launching a new field report

 

Next, you will be asked to enter the report details including:

1.       Title:  select a descriptive title that will makes sense for your dealership

2.       Product Code:  indicate the deficient product or component.  If you are not sure, enter your best guess and continue.  This report will need to be attached to a product code related to the original Ack # in the dealer portal.

3.       Reason:  select a reason code from the drop down

4.       Quantity Affected:  enter the quantity of product deficient

5.       Problem Description:  enter a detailed description of the deficiency.  Be as specific as possible

6.       Photos:  Tap the icon of the camera to take a photo of the deficiency.  Repeat this step to attach multiple photos

Tapping the <Back indicator will save the field report.

Figure 34:  Providing field report details

Once you have captured a photo, you can add a caption or a voiceover, as well as edit your photo in order to further support your claim.

Figure 35:  Adding detail to field report photos

To provide additional information related to a photo captured on the mobile app, you can:

1.       Add caption:  tap here to add beneath your photo

2.       Tap the image of the microphone to record audio describing your issue

3.       Tap the image of the megaphone to listen to previously recorded messages

4.       Tap the pencil to edit the photo.  Options for editing include:

a.       Enhancing image detail

b.      Adjusting brightness, contrast, warmth and saturation

c.       Cropping the image

d.      Drawing freehand on the image (i.e. circle the problematic area)

e.      Orientation changes

f.        Adding colour effects

g.       Adding text over the image itself

 

Figure 36:  Example photo editing

 

The mobile app will also allow you to manage your field reports from the main page.  By tapping each claim, you can edit the details previously provided.  These changes will be immediately available to any user accessing the field report via the mobile app or the web based dealer portal. 

If the field report is no longer required, you can archive it by swiping the report to the left tapping Archive.

Administrative Functions

The Teknion Dealer Portal gives dealers the ability to assign new users who can create, edit, submit and manage claims, claim requests and field reports on behalf of the dealership.  Every dealership will need to select at least one staff member who will be given this administrative role.  Dealers will be responsible for any information or guidance provided via the Dealer Portal for any users associated with the dealership.

To access the User Admin features, click on the Tools menu at the top, right-hand corner of the Dealer Portal.  Select User Admin.

Figure 38:  Accessing User Admin features

You should now see a list of the users that are associated with your dealership.  Clicking on their login name will give you access to the users profile, where you can set their access levels.

If you need to create a new user, click the New User button at the top of the All Users screen.  Enter the user details, as indicated on the New Profile box (See Figure 39) and click save to create the new user.

Figure 39:  Creating a new user

 

You can also manage your own profile by clicking on your username at the top, right-hand corner of the Dealer Portal screens.

 

Figure 40:  Accessing your user profile

If you are established at multiple dealer locations (i.e. multiple dealer offices with multiple Teknion accounts), you can select which account you wish to work in by clicking the appropriate Account.

To change your profile settings, simply click Profile and follow the on screen prompts.
